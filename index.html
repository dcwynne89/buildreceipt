📌 Receipt Tips That Actually Make a Difference

Always issue a receipt — even for cash

Cash transactions leave no paper trail. A receipt protects both parties — the payer has proof of payment, and you have proof you received it. This is especially important for deposits and partial payments.

Include the payment method and reference number

If a customer disputes a charge six months from now, the reference number ties your receipt to a specific transaction. "Bank Transfer" is good; "Bank Transfer — TXN-20260607-001" is bulletproof.

Itemize every charge — don't lump-sum

A receipt that says "$500 — Services" raises more questions than it answers. Break it down: "2 hrs Consultation @ $150, Material Costs $200." Clarity prevents callbacks.

Use sequential receipt numbers

RCT-001, RCT-002, RCT-003. Sequential numbering makes it easy to spot gaps, track volume, and look professional if you're ever audited. Start with 001 and never skip.
